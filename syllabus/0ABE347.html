授業概要

国際関係の主要な理論を、英語文献を通して学習する。国際政治における対立と協調に関する論文を取り上げ、様々な角度から諸理論・アプローチを理解することにとどまらず、実際のケースに理論を応用する能力を身に付ける。また、理論の政策的なインプリケーションについても検討し、理論が国際関係を理解する上で、どの程度役立つかあるいは妨げとなるかも検証する。

授業の到達目標(学修成果)

Goals of the Course: Through studying various international political issues and major International Relations theories, students will be able to acquire "expertise," and "research ability" needed to understand how theories can be applied. Students will also improve “communication ability,” broaden their “global awareness,” and learn the significance of the "capacity to leverage academic knowledge."

成績評価方法

Grading:
Grades will be based on classroom participation (20%), presentation (30%), and a term paper (50%).

About the term paper:

Pick one of the readings in this course and expand. If you are interested in the topic you presented, that is fine. If you want to write about the topic others have presented, that is fine, too.

First, briefly summarize the argument, and then provide critical comments. You are welcome to refer to sources other than the articles we read in class.

Due: 11th week of class (17:00)

Submit to: ohtomo.takafumi.gf@u.tsukuba.ac.jp      

Length: 5 pages

Format (Please follow carefully):

-Font: Times New Roman preferred
-Font size: 12
-23-25 lines per page
-2.5 cm margin (top, bottom, left, and right)
-include page numbers (bottom center)
-include bibliography (references): Chicago style, APA, or MLA.
4-page paper plus 1-page bibliography→X
    5-page paper plus 1-page bibliography→O

教材・参考文献・配付資料等

(1) Organizational Meeting and Introduction

(2) International Relations Theory: An Introduction
Stephen Walt, "International Relations: One World, Many Theories," Foreign Policy, no. 110 (Spring, 1998): 29-32, 34-46.

(3) The Rise and Fall of the International Liberal Order
John Mearsheimer, "Bound to Fail: The Rise and Fall of the International Liberal Order," International Security 43, no. 4 (Spring 2019): 7-50.

(4) Nuclear Taboo
Nina Tannenwald, “The Nuclear Taboo: The United States and the Normative Basis of Nuclear Non-Use,” International Organization 53, no. 3 (Summer 1999): 433-468.

(5) Extended Nuclear Deterrence
Matthew Fuhrmann and Todd S. Sechser, "Signaling Alliance Commitments: Hand-Tying and Sunk Costs in Extended Deterrence," American Journal of Political Science 58, no. 4 (October 2014): 919-935.

(6) Chinese Views on Nuclear Escalation
Fiona S. Cunningham and M. Taylor Fravel, “Dangerous Confidence? Chinese Views on Nuclear Escalation," International Security 44, no. 2 (Fall 2019): 61-109.

(7) NATO Non-extension
Marc Trachtenberg, "The United States and the NATO Non-extension Assurances of 1990: New Light on an Old Problem?" International Security 45, no. 3 (Winter 2020/21): 162-203.

(8) Weaponized Interdependence
Henry Farrell and Abraham L. Newman, "Weaponized Interdependence: How Global Economic Networks Shape State Coercion," International Security 44, no. 1 (Summer 2019): 42-79.

(9)Cyber Operations
Lennart Maschmeyer, "The Subversive Trilemma: Why Cyber Operations Fall Short of Expectations," International Security 46, no. 2 (Fall 2021): 51-90.

(10) Declining Hegemon Doesn’t Always Fight
Paul K. MacDonald and Joseph M. Parent, “Graceful Decline? The Surprising Success of Great Power Retrenchment,” International Security 35, no. 4 (Spring 2011): 7-44.
