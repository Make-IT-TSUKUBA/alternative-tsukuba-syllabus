Students will become more competent in intercultural communication skills as they expand and use vocabulary connected to concepts of cultural anthropology. 授業計画

This course focuses on developing reading and listening skills, while employing a topical focus on the basic concepts of cultural anthropology.  We will examine the solutions that different cultures and societies provide to problems humans face. Our academic goal is to better understand and to be able to describe in English the nature of interactions between different peoples and cultures. Tasks will include taking notes in academic contexts, summarizing material, and finding key points. Topics include: norms/values; ethnocentrism/cultural relativism; cultural universals/cultural particulars; social organization.

成績評価方法

1. Participation, discussion, and in-class activities 30%
2. Assignments 30%
3. Final exam 40%

Students will demonstrate understanding of course readings and information presented in class through discussions, activities, reflection papers, and online examination. There will be two homework modules, each worth 15 points (for a total of 30 points).

学修時間の割り当て及び授業外における学修方法

Each class will include lecture and discussion. Weekly assignments connect to topics presented and discussed in class and will be turned in at two points in the term as homework modules. The assignments include reading reflections, completing tasks on websites, and an interview that you will conduct.
