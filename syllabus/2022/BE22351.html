BE22351   Introduction to International Law

1.0 単位, 1 - 4 年次, 秋AB 月3
秋山 肇

授業概要

This course introduces the basics of international law. Students learn history and traditional topics as well as recent issues in international law.

備考

This course welcomes non-native English speaking students of both School of Social and International Studies and other schools and programmes.
英語で授業。
オンライン(同時双方向型)

授業方法

講義

学位プログラム・コンピテンスとの関係

This course is designed to develop the following competencies.

General-propose competencies
- Communication ability
- Critical and creative thinking skills
- Internationalism and broad views of things
- Team-working, independence, autonomy

Special competencies
- Understanding international relations
- Multidisciplinary Knowledge
- Fundamentals of negotiation skills
- Policy-related fundamental skills
- Cross-cultural awareness skills
- Project analysis and completion skills

授業の到達目標（学修成果）

1. Be able to understand the basics of international law, and analyse international issues from the perspective of international law.
2. Be able to identify the significance and limitation of international law.

キーワード

International Law, Constitutional Law, International Society, United Nations, Peace and Security, Territory, Sea, Human Rights, Environment, Critical Legal Studies, SDGs, Global Issues, Tutorial

授業計画

The contents of this course are subject to change upon consultation with students.
Part I “Invitation to International Law”: Lessons 1-2
Part II “Basics of International Law”: Lessons 3-4
Part III “Issues”: Lessons 5-10

1) Introduction
2) Introduction to International Society and Law
3) Sources of International Law
4) States and Other Actors in International Law
5) Peace and Security
6) International Crimes
7) Territory and Sea
8) Human Rights: International Law and Constitutional Law
9) Environmental Issues: International Law and SDGs
10) Wrap-up Discussion: Global Issues and International Law

履修条件

Students should have a passion for learning international law in English. Prior learning experiences in English is not required.

成績評価方法

Contribution to discussion (20%), Weekly assignment (30%), 1000-word Short Final Paper (50%)  
The final grade will be finalised referring to student's self-evaluation.
This may change depends on the number of students upon consultation with them.

学修時間の割り当て及び授業外における学修方法

This course consists of lecture (about 60%) and discussion (about 40%).
Pursuant to Article 30 of the Undergraduate School Rules of the University of Tsukuba, one credit requires 45 hours of study. Thus, students are expected to study 3 hours outside of the class in addition to the class (about 1.5 hours) each week.
Students are expected to read relevant articles and textbooks and prepare a Short Final Paper outside of the class.
