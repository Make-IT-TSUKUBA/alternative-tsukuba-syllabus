BE22471   Globalization and Development

2.0 単位, 2 - 4 年次, 秋AB 火2,金2

授業概要

This course examines the politics of development in the era of globalization, exploring major issues and problems being encountered and confronted by the developing countries in the contemporary period.  The aims of the course are:
• To broaden students’ knowledge of major development issues and the impact of globalization on developing countries.                                                                                
• To provide students with a good introduction to globalization theory and to the politics of development.

授業計画

1) October 1 - Introduction
2) October 5 - The concept of globalization (Steger, Chapters 1-2)
Guide questions: Do you think that glocalization is a positive or a negative development? Is globalization mainly an economic, political, cultural or environmental phenomenon? Explain briefly.
3) October 8 - The economic dimension of globalization (Steger, Chapter 3); The developing in the global economy (Burnell et al., Chapter 4)
Guide questions: Has economic globalization had a positive or a negative economic impact on developing countries? Has the economic rise of China had a positive or negative impact on the global economy?
4) October 12 - The political dimension of globalization (Steger, Chapter 4);
The developing world in international politics (Burnell et al., Chapter 5)
Guide questions: Has political globalization diminished or curtailed the power of the nation-state? Has globalization had a positive or a negative political
impact on developing countries?
5) October 15 - The cultural dimension of globalization (Steger, Chapter 5);
Ideological confrontations over globalization (Steger, Chapter 7)
Guide questions: Has globalization brought about greater sameness or difference in cultures of different countries? Is this a good or a bad development? What
has globalization to do with the worldwide rise of populism? Is this good or bad?
6) October 19 - Changing analytical approaches to the study of politics in thedeveloping world (Burnell et al., Chapter 1)
Guide questions: According to Vicky Randall, the main approaches to the study of politics in the developing world are the theories of modernization, dependency and globalization. With which of these three approaches do you tend to agree? Why? Do you agree or disagree with Edward Said's concept of "orientalism"? Why?
7) October 22 - The ecological dimension of globalization (Steger, Chapter 6;
Burnell et al., pp. 259-265)
Guide questions: Why are ecological effects of globalization said to be the most significant and potentially life threatening for the world? Why have many developing countries not been too successful in working towards sustainable development?
8) October 26 - Colonialism and post-colonial development (Burnell et al., Chapter 2)
Guide questions: What has been the impact of colonialism on today's developing countries? Why has it been difficult for former colonies to turn themselves
into strong and stable nation-states?
9) October 29 - The modern state (Burnell et al., Chapter 12)
Guide questions: How would you compare the despotic power and the infrastructural power of states in the developing world? What should be done to help weak states become stronger?
10) November 2 - Development (Burnell  et al., Chapter 16);
Guide questions: What does development really mean and what should a developing country’s development objectives be? Why have some developing countries been more successful than others in achieving development objectives?
11) November 12 - Quiz game I
12) November 16 - Inequality (Burnell et al., Chapter 6)
Guide questions: Why is global inequality growing? Does growth inevitably lead to inequality? If so, does it matter as long as poverty declines?
13) November 19 - Women and gender (Burnell et al., Chapter 9)
Guide questions: Should women in all developing countries have equal political rights and civil liberties with men irrespective of culture, religious beliefs, and tradition? Why are some developing countries more successful in promoting gender equality than others?
14) November 25 - Ethnopolitics and nationalism (Burnell et al., Chapter 7)
Guide questions: Why is ethnic/ethnopolitical conflict still strong in some developing countries? Is nationalism good or bad for developing countries?
15) December 3 - Religion (Burnell et al., Chapter 8)
Guide questions: In your view, what is the relationship of religion and democratization in the developing world? In the light of secularization in many developed countries, what do you think about political Islam?
16) December 7 - Civil society (Burnell et al., Chapter 10); Social movements
and alternative politics (Burnell et al., Chapter 11)
Guide questions: Is civil society strong or weak in developing countries? Why?
What are the strengths and challenges of social movements in the developing world?
17) December 10 - Democratization and Regime Change (Burnell et al., Chapter
14)
Guide questions: Has the world been experiencing more of democratization or of democratic regresssion since the turn of the millennium? What accounts for this trend? Certain governments have contended that "democracy promotion" has just served as an pretext for foreign intervention and regime change. Do you agree or disagree? Explain briefly.
18) December 14 - Violent conflict and intervention (Burnell et al., Chapter 13)
Guide questions: Compare violent concflicts in the Cold War and post-Cold War contexts. In what ways have they changed, or not changed? In your view, what
has been the impact of the "war on terror" on developing countries?
19) December 17 - Human rights (Burnell et al., Chapter 18)
Guide questions: Is concern for human rights a help or a hindrance for development policies? The idea of the universalism of human rights has been put
into question in the so-called "Asian values" debate. Certain Asian leaders (Mahathir and Lee Kuan Yew) have advanced the view that the dominant interpretation of human rights is much too influenced by Western ideals of individualism and liberalism, which run counter to Asian values of
collectivism, harmony and respect for authority. What is your position in this debate?
20) December 21 - International migration (Hein de Haas, Stephen Castles and Mark Miller); The future of globalization (Steger, chapter 8)
Guide questions: Does migration promote or hinder development in developing countries? Why? With which of the three possible future scenarios of globalization do you tend to agree? Why?
21) December 24 - Quiz game II
22) December 28 - Final exam
