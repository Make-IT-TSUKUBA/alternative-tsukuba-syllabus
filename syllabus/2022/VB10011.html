VB10011   Methodology for Global Issues

3.0 単位, 1 年次, 秋AB 水2-4
森尾 貴広, 野村 名可男, ジャクタ ブルノ, サンドバール フェリーぺ, EOM SUNYONG, BPGI関係教員

授業概要

Using the Problem Based Learning Approach, in this course students can deepen the global issues at three levels:
1. deepen the knowledge and the information about a problem, issue
2. how everyone is concerned (I, you, them) and how everything is linked
3. what behaviour to adopt, what to do to tackle the global issues, looking globally to problem and identifying local solutions

授業の到達目標（学修成果）

The academic goal of this course is to achieve three levels of learning objectives, which were determined by the UNESCO in the «Education for Sustainable Development Goals – Learning Objectives» such as:
- Cognitive learning objectives
- Socio-emotional learning objectives
- Behavioral learning objectives

キーワード

global issues, Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), cognitive learning objectives, socio-emotional learning objectives, behavioural learning objectives

授業計画

第1講 [Academic writing] Wednesday 2 periods Fall AB (Jactat, B)
In this part, students will acquire the basic understanding and skills in academic writing and presentation veered toward global issues.
Introduction
1. Units 1-3 CUBING & POWER systems for writing. The Self-introduction paragraph.
Writing paragraphs
2. Unit 4 Descriptive Paragraph [Describe It]
3. Unit 5 Comparison and Contrast Paragraph [Compare It]
    Unit 6 Process Paragraph [Narrate It]
4. Unit 7 Narrative Paragraph [Narrate It]
    Unit 8 Summary Paragraph [Summarize It]
5. Unit 9 Analysis Paragraph [Explain It]
    Unit 10 Cause and Effect Paragraph [Explain It]
6. Unit 11 Persuasive Paragraph [Argue It]
     Unit 12 Problem-Solution Paragraph [Argue It]
Writing an Essay
7. Unit 15 Writing an Essay from the Beginning
8. Unit 16 Review Essay: Combining Approaches
From Written Essay to Oral Presentation
9. Preparing for the Oral Presentation
10. Delivering an Oral Presentation
第2講 [Approaches from environmental aspects] Wednesday 3-4 periods Fall A (Nomura, N., Eom, S.)
In this part, students will experience how to make your own data and search secondary data from various resources. They will also learn how to illustrate the data in understandable and objective ways.
1-2. Environmental issues and geographic information system
3-4. Foundation of GIS analysis for global issues
5-6. Visualizing the spatial data
7-8. Application of GIS for understanding global issues
9.   Utilizing geospatial information to implement SDGs
10.     Students Presentations  
第3講 [Approaches from human aspects] Wednesday 3-4 periods Fall B (Aoki S., Sandoval, F.)
1-2.  Introduction, Tackle Global Issues
3-4.  Global Compact Networking    
5-6.  Approach to SDGs  (History, Methodology)
7-8.  SDGs and Global society
9.     SDGs and Social environment
10.    Students Presentations
