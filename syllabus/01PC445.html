Course Objectives(Learning Outcomes)

Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) has become a buzzword in international business. Most major companies are engaging in CSR activities and are publishing reports to document their various commitments. CSR activities address a broad range of issues such as environment, energy, safety, diversity, human rights, accountability, compliance and governance, and can take on many forms ‐cause promotion, cause-related marketing, philanthropy, community, volunteering, sponsorship, socially responsible business practices and social business.

The purpose of our class is to analyze the CSR activities of globally operating Japanese and German companies to work out culture-bound differences, identify best practices and point out opportunities for future actions. The analysis will focus on two major questions:

- What issues do companies emphasize and what forms of engagement do they apply?
- To what extent do they align their CSR activates with their corporate strategy both at the global and local level.
