授業概要

These courses will provide experiences of tackling global issues. Students will conduct, individually or on a team, a practical activity in order to understand causes, backgrounds, processes of the global issues and seek solutions. Students also use a variety of methods for this purpose, including group discussions, data collection, field research, and experimentation. It will be important to derive feasible solutions based on objective evidences. In the end, students will make a presentation of the research and obtain critical feedback on the project.

学位プログラム・コンピテンスとの関係

In the diploma policy of BPGI, students are required to have the following knowledge and abilities:
(1) A broad knowledge of global issues from the perspective of integrating the humanities and sciences.
(2) Acquire a systematic specialized knowledge of global issues and the ability to analyze them comprehensively from multiple perspectives and to work on solving them with creativity and ingenuity.
(3) The ability to communicate logically and persuasively to express one's own views in a global society, while also being flexible enough to listen to the opinions of others.
(4) Demonstrate leadership in a cross-disciplinary and cross-cultural environment and have the ability to contribute to society.
(5) High ethical standards in a global society and the ability to understand diversity.

This course is designed to cultivate the above competencies (1) (2), (3) and (4).

授業の到達目標（学修成果）

The purposes of undertaking a practical training are to give you:
- An opportunity to enhance skills of problem formation and solution development to attack global issues regarding an area in which you have a special interest
- An opportunity to learn broad knowledge from the presentation of other students and discussions
- Experience in managing a project

成績評価方法

Students are requested to submit the following reports, revised study plans and presentation files to manaba prior to the class. The respective submission deadlines must be strictly observed.
Presentation file of
- Reflection of the previous study projects
- Idea presentation 1st
- Idea presentation 2nd
- Final presentation
- Study Project (Form 1) The proposal should be revised and amended as appropriate.
- Final Report (Form 2) The report should be revised and amended as appropriate.

Each presentation is evaluated as follows.
1. Reflection of the previous study projects 15%
- Clear introduction of the previous research topic 5%
- Logical and clear reflection of the previous study project 5%
- Appropriate response to questions and comments: 5%
2. Idea presentation 1st 15%
- Clear and logical explanation of the topic including its background, motivation, significance and impact to deal with the proposed topic 10%
- Appropriate response to questions and comments: 5%
3. Idea presentation 2nd 25%
- Clear and logical explanation of the topic including its background, motivation, significance and impact to deal with the proposed topic 10%
- Clear and logical explanation of literature review and ‘gap’ in the literature 10%
- Appropriate response to questions and comments: 5%
4. Final presentation 25%.
- Clear and logical explanation of research question including the background, significance, relationship with the ‘gap’ in the literature and the impact on the global (and local) society.  20%
- Appropriate response to questions and comments: 5%
5. Topic report 15%.
- Clear and logical explanation of research question including the background, significance, relationship with the ‘gap’ in the literature and the impact on the global (and local) society.  15%
6. Contribution to the class 5%.
- Active participation in question and answer sessions and discussions in class.  5%
