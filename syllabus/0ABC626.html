0ABC626   国際機構論演習

1.0 単位, 1・2 年次, 秋AB 木2
秋山 肇

授業概要

The purpose of this seminar is to enhance students' ability to conduct independent research on international organisations. Students will be able to identify their own academic interests, produce a clear research plan, and write an academic paper on international organisations.

備考

西暦奇数年度開講。
英語で授業。
授業形式はシラバスの「その他」欄で確認してください。

授業方法

演習

学位プログラム・コンピテンスとの関係

This seminar is designed to develop the following competences.

General-propose competences
- Communicative ability
- Team work

Degree program competences
- Research capability
- Professional knowledge
- Ethics

授業の到達目標(学修成果)

1. Be able to analyse the role and limitations of international organisations to deal with a particular global issue which each student is interested.
2. Be able to conduct an independent research on international organisations with critical perspectives.

キーワード

International Organisations, United Nations, Peace, Security, Human Rights, SDGs, Regional Organisations, Research Method, Research Writing, tutorial

授業計画

The contents of this course are subject to change upon consultation with students.

第1回 Introduction
第2回 What are International Organisations?
第3回 Research Method on International Organisations
第4回 Clarification of Interest
第5回 Analysis of Relevant Sources
第6回 Examination of a Research Plan
第7回 Peer-reading of the First Draft (1000 words)
第8回 Peer-reading of the Second Draft (2000 words)
第9回 Peer-reading of the Final Draft (2500 words)
第10回 Final Presentation

履修条件

Students should have a passion for learning international organisations in English. Prior learning experiences in English is not required.

成績評価方法

Contribution to discussion (20%), Weekly assignment including Research Plan (30%), 2500-word Final Short Paper (50%)

学修時間の割り当て及び授業外における学修方法

This course consists of student presentations (about 30%) and discussion (about 70%).
Pursuant to Article 33 of the Graduate School Rules of the University of Tsukuba, one credit requires 45 hours of study. Thus, students are expected to study six hours outside of the class in addition to the class (about three hours) each week.
Students are expected to prepare a Final Short Paper outside of the class.
