BC12342   開発と金融

2.0 単位, 2 - 4 年次, 春AB 木3,4
鈴木 英明

授業概要

This course will discuss what modality of finance will be needed for achieving 2030 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), based on the understanding that conventional types of development finance such as ODAs will be vastly inadequate for SDGs. The discussion will take up a variety of new financing modalities for development, taking into account ongoing works at global forum such as the World Bank. This course will be conducted in English. Students need to expect heavy workload in terms of reading requirement and class presentation.

学位プログラム・コンピテンスとの関係

This course will discuss what modality of finance will be needed for achieving 2030 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), based on the understanding that conventional types of development finance such as ODAs will be vastly inadequate for SDGs. The discussion will take up a variety of new financing modalities for development, taking into account ongoing works at global forum such as the World Bank. This course may be beneficial to those students who major in International Development.

授業の到達目標（学修成果）

Students are expected to gain the ability to understand and analyze a variety of financing modalities available for economic development.

キーワード

Sustainable development goals, economic development, ODA, tax system, expenditure reform, PPP, private investment, international financial institutions.

授業計画

Week 1: Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)
Week 2: Student presentation
Week 3: Domestic resource mobilization & Public expenditure reform
Week 4: Student presentation
Week 5: ODA & Private finance (investment climate, microfinance, etc.)
Week 6: Student presentation
Week 7: Public Private Partnership
Week 8: Student presentation
Week 9: Remittances, Innovative Finance & Role of international financial institutions
Week 10: Student Presentation

履修条件

Prerequisite: This course requires very basic knowledge on how the government and companies raise funds they need for investments. In Session 1, a placement test will be carried out to certify that students have such knowledge. Only those students who have demonstrated to have required knowledge in the placement test will be admitted to the class and given course credits.

成績評価方法

1)  Placement test (20 points) in week 1
2)  Five class presentations (7 points each, 35 points in total)
3)  Five class questions (3 points each, 15 points)
4)  Final quiz (30 points)
