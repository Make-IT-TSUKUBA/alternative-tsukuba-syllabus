授業概要

This lecture serves as an introduction to bachelor's course education at the university. This course invites students to learn about academic methodology at the university level, and cultivates an understanding of the academic field in which they are majoring, as well as the ability to understand the relationship with related fields.

授業の到達目標(学修成果)

・To be able to explain the relationship between academic disciplines closely related to one's own intellectual curiosity and awareness of issues, and related disciplines in the surrounding area, and to be able to make an appropriate course plan in relation to this.
・To be able to explain in detail your own selection policy and process that led to your choice of  academic field.

The above are required. The following are further objectives expected.
・To be able to explain the academic methodology practiced at a university in contrast to the way of studying before entering a university, and to be able to start learning in accordance with the academic methodology at a university.
・To be able to explain, by citing actual examples, that the various kinds of knowledge acquired at a university are backed by the tireless thought and practice of our predecessors. Hence, to be able to examine from multiple perspectives how knowledge connects related knowledge and how knowledge is linked.

授業計画

　This lecture will be conducted online (Asynchronous) using the manaba course "Invitation to Arts and Sciences(lectures)". Check the news or announcements in the course frequently.
　This course consists of an introductory lecture and 9 lectures introducing the academic content of the Schools, which can be viewed via lecture videos.

・ Introductory lecture (the origins of academic discipline and the perspective of problem solving)
　By pointing out the similarities and differences between what students learn before entering university and what they learn at university, and by introducing some of the ways in which "learning" is viewed, we will show that learning begins with asking one's own questions, and that it is a process of seeking answers to those questions. We will also explain the philosophy and significance of liberal arts education at the university, and present an overall picture of the educational policy and model, inviting students to enter the world of learning and study at the university.
・ Lectures introducing the School
　Explain the significance of the academic problems that are mainly covered by each School and College, and introduce how the academic disciplines of each School and College cover the approaches to solving those problems. In particular, if there are any achievements in which the university has made significant contributions to society, these will also be introduced. The flow of the explanation should be problem → solution → field →Schools/Colleges. Interrelationships with other Schools and Colleges will be introduced as necessary. By attending each lecture, students will be able to see the whole picture of the academic research being conducted at the university, and will be able to have a bird's eye view of the academic field they have chosen, and will be able to look at the field from a variety of perspectives.

　The introductory lecture and each School lecture will be available Online(Asynchronous) from October 3, 15:15 to November 9, 23:59. The introductory lecture must be watched first. After watching the introductory lecture, students are free to watch the lectures in any order.

The content of this lecture will also be relevant to this course. Please connect these to your own study.
