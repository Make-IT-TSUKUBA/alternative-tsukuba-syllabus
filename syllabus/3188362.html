授業の到達目標(学修成果)

The purpose of this elective course is to provide students with the necessary tools to design and deliver complete and sound academic presentations in English, and with confidence.

Students will engage in activities of presentation design and learn different ways of storytelling and how to frame their presentations depending on their purpose, as well as how to design individual parts of presentations such as the introduction and conclusion.

Technical academic communication techniques will be introduced, in order for students to learn how to present scientific topics with clarity, precision and conciseness. These include the And But Therefore (ABT) approach, the old/new contract, and the paramedic method.

This course will showcase various examples of presentation styles, and students will have opportunities to identify, analyze, and discuss different elements of successful presentations.

Finally, this course will require students to engage in presentations of different purposes, time lengths, and formality, putting their knowledge to practice and enhancing their confidence for their different presentations of their academic life.

授業計画

This class has a 15-week schedule. The details are as follows:

第1回 Orientation, course introduction, self-introductions
第2回 Knowing your audience.
Transdisciplinarity and the science communicator challenge.
Three audiences: other scientists (same discipline), other scientists (different discipline), non scientists.
第3回 Storytelling and framing.
And, But, Therefore (ABT)in:
History, politics, academic presentations and science communication.
第4回 Topic 1: Artificial Intelligence (AI). History and definitions. Artificial General Intelligence vs Narrow AI.
Presentation analysis: Sam Harris: Can we build AI without losing control over it?
第5回 Topic 1: Artificial Intelligence (AI) continued. The Turing Test and the Uncanny Valley (不気味の谷).
The 6 sentence argument: how to create a position and thesis statement in only 6 sentences.
2 minute presentation: Should we, or should we not develop machines that pass the Turing Test and the Uncanny Valley?
第6回 Topic 2: Nuclear power. Definitions and history of nuclear power in the world. Global nuclear power adoption statistics.
Presentation analysis: Michael Shellenberger: How fear of nuclear power is hurting the environment.
第7回 Topic 2: Nuclear power continued. The International Nuclear Event (INES) Scale.
History of major nuclear accidents. The coincidence of the China syndrome.
6 sentence argument and 2 minute presentation: Should we, or should we not continue the development of nuclear power?
第8回 Topic 3: Social Media. Global statistics of Social Media adoption.
Presentation analysis: Tristan Harris: How a handful of tech companies control billions of minds every day.
第9回 Topic 3: Social Media continued. Social media engagement, compare and despair, loneliness at school.
Debate speech analysis: Jonathan Haidt: Has social media destroyed a generation?
2 minute presentation: At what age should minors be granted access to social media?
第10回 Topic 3: Social Media continued. Lost focus and the importance of sleep for our health. The concept of cruel optimism.
Presentation analysis: Matthew Walker. Sleep is your superpower.
第11回 Introduction, Methodology, Results and Discussion (IMRaD), and the old/new contract.
2 minute presentation: Describe a concept from your discipline using IMRaD and the old/new contract.
第12回 Topic 4: The psychology of evil. The Stanford prison experiment and the incident in Abu Ghraib prison. The seven social processes towards evil actions.
Presentation analysis: Philip Zimbardo: The psychology of evil.
第13回 Topic 5: Enlightenment now. The concepts of progress, reason and humanism. Global statistics of human progress.
Presentation analysis: Steven Pinker: Is the world getting better or worse? A look at the numbers.
第14回 Becoming the audience: Active listening.
Final topics: Freedom of speech, critical thinking, and John Stuart Mill’s three rules.
Presentation summary through active listening: Yeonmi Park: What I learned about freedom after escaping North Korea.
第15回 Final Presentations
