授業概要

国際関係の主要な理論を、英語文献を通して学習する。国際政治における対立と協調に関する論文を取り上げ、様々な角度から諸理論・アプローチを批判的に検証することを通し、社会科学的な分析能力を身に付ける。また、理論検証に関する文献も読むことを通し、理論全般における理解を深める。

備考

西暦偶数年度開講。

授業の到達目標(学修成果)

Goals of the Course: Through studying various international political issues and major International Relations theories, students will be able to acquire "expertise," and "research ability" needed to understand how theories can be applied. Students will also improve “communication ability,” broaden their “global awareness,” and learn the significance of the "capacity to leverage academic knowledge."

成績評価方法

Grading:
Grades will be based on a term paper (50%), and active classroom participation (50%).

About the term paper:

Pick one of the readings in this course and expand. If you are interested in the topic you presented, that is fine. If you want to write about the topic others have presented, that is fine, too.

First, briefly summarize the argument, and then provide critical comments. You are welcome to refer to sources other than the articles we read in class.

Due: 11th week of class (17:00)

Submit to: ohtomo.takafumi.gf@u.tsukuba.ac.jp      

Length: 5 pages

Format (Please follow carefully):

-Font: Times New Roman preferred
-Font size: 12
-23-25 lines per page
-2.5 cm margin (top, bottom, left, and right)
-include page numbers (bottom center)
-include bibliography (references): Chicago style, APA, or MLA.
4-page paper plus 1-page bibliography→X
    5-page paper plus 1-page bibliography→O

教材・参考文献・配付資料等

(1) Organizational Meeting and Introduction

(2) Realism
--John Mearsheimer, “Back to the Future: Instability in Europe after the Cold War,” International Security 15, no. 1 (Summer 1990): 5-56.


(3) Liberal Theory
--G. John Ikenberry, “Strategic Restraint, and the Persistence of American Postwar Order,” International Security 23, No. 3 (Winter, 1998/99): 43-78.

(4) Unipolarity

(7) The Rise of China

(8) Base Politics
--Kent Calder, Embattled Garrisons: Comparative Base Politics and American Globalism (Princeton: Princeton University Press, 2007), chap 6.

(9) Asia and International Relations theory
--David C. Kang, “Getting Asia Wrong: The Need for New Analytical Framework,” International Security 27, no. 4 (Spring 2003): 57-85.

(10) Summary
