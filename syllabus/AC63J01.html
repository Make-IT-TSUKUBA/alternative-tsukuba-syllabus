授業計画

Overshadowed by ecological disasters, this class looks at new theoretical ways to reinvent our concepts of our own lives and of the network planet we are living on. How can a new view of our lives and material and discursive foundations help finding solutions for the future?
This class focuses on the thinking and writing of Donna Haraway. It offers text excerpts from her and various other authors to learn about post-humanist revisions of our planet.
Basic instruction mode is reading passages and interpreting them. Students are
expected to read and prepare texts (vocabulary) before class and to prepare
homework when given and to present it in class. Students are expected to actively
perform in class discussions.

第1回 Introduction to the class: Who are we/what is us? Where do we live and when?
第2回 Organization of the class: living syllabi – where/how do we go from here?
第3回 Theoretical background: post-trans-meta-hu-man-ism (text excerpts)
第4回 Excerpts from “Posthuman Studies Reader”: critical post-post-humanism
第5回 Literary Example: Le Guin
第6回 Focusing on Donna Haraway I: Excerpts from “Simians, Cyborgs, and Women”
第7回 Focusing on Donna Haraway II: Excerpts from “Simians, Cyborgs, and Women”
第8回 Focusing on Donna Haraway III: Anthropocene vs. Chthulucene
第9回 Selected themes (students), writing, and outlook I
第10回 Selected themes (students), writing, and outlook II

If necessity requires, schedules may be changed.

履修条件

The class is on an introductory-intermediate level.
English for discussion, academic writing and reading.

成績評価方法

Weekly class performance will be graded and most important (60% of overall grade). Final paper (レポート) (length depending on student's proficiency in English) and/or oral presentation (depending on size of class) will be graded (30% of overall grade).
Homework (when given) and class preparations (connecting learning elements) will
be graded (10% of overall grade). Absence from class follows general regulations.

学修時間の割り当て及び授業外における学修方法

The course consists of lectures/reading (30%) and discussions/understanding of material (70%). Students are expected to prepare lessons (homework and otherwise) and to revise learning contents on their own. The film viewing will take place outside of class lessons.
