VB10012   Seminars on Global Issues III

6.0 単位, 4 年次, 秋ABC 応談
森尾 貴広, サンドバール フェリーぺ

授業概要

The aim of this series of seminars is to enable each student to determine his/her research theme on specific issues at the global or planetary scale. At this level, students are supervised and directed individually to better enable each one to develop his/her own original approach and be able to contact the appropriate laboratories at the University of Tsukuba.

備考

Students who have completed the Seminars on Global Issues A-II (VB20022) and Seminars on Global Issues B-II (VB30023) .

学位プログラム・コンピテンスとの関係

In the diploma policy of  BPGI, students are required to have the following knowledge and abilities:
(1) A broad knowledge of global issues from the perspective of integrating the humanities and sciences.
(2) Acquire a systematic specialized knowledge of global issues and the ability to analyze them comprehensively from multiple perspectives and to work on solving them with creativity and ingenuity.
(3) The ability to communicate logically and persuasively to express one's own views in a global society, while also being flexible enough to listen to the opinions of others.
(4) Demonstrate leadership in a cross-disciplinary and cross-cultural environment and have the ability to contribute to society.
(5) High ethical standards in a global society and the ability to understand diversity.

This course is designed to cultivate the above competencies (1), (2), (3), and (5) in combination with Graduation Research.

Students who have completed the Seminars on Global Issues A-II (VB20022) and Seminars on Global Issues B-II (VB30023) .

成績評価方法

These courses will be evaluated based on progress of graduation thesis by advisor professor.

学修時間の割り当て及び授業外における学修方法学修時間の割り当て及び授業外における学修方法（本授業は1単位当たり45時間の学修時間が必要です。対面授業と合わせ以下に記載する授業外学修を行ってください。）

This course will be conducted under the guidance of academic advisors as needed. Outside of the class, students will organize their knowledge of previous research and critically interpret it through literature reviews conducted by the students themselves.
Note that one credit course requires 30 hours of self-study outside of class in addition to 15 hours of contact hours.
