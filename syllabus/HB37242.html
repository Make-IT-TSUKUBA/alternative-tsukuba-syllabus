授業概要

This course focuses on preparing students who are interested in taking the Occupational English Test (OET) for Medicine, which is currently the standard English language proficiency test for medical professionals who want to practice medicine in an English-speaking country (UK, Australia, USA, etc.). The main goal is to give students a chance to familiarize themselves with the test format, with a special focus on the Writing Section. Another goal of the course is to improve students' clinical communication skills in general.

授業の到達目標(学修成果)

At the end of this course, students will feel more confident about
1. being able to take the Occupational English Test (Medicine)
2. understanding conversations in healthcare settings
3. grasping the important points in written medical communications
4. writing a referral letter in English
5. speaking with a patient whose first language is English

キーワード

Occupational English Test (Medicine), OET, medical communication skills

授業計画

Students will gain the confidence to tackle the OET (Medicine) test which is required when applying to work in healthcare settings in an English-speaking countries. Students will be provided with tips for improving the skills needed for the Reading and Listening components. The main focus of the course will be on the Writing and Speaking components. For the Writing component, students will practice writing a referral letter. For the Speaking component, students will hone their clinical communication skills for talking with patients.

Grades are based on work throughout the course. The grade will be calculated as a total of your GoogleForm assignments as follows
1. 10% Unit 1
2. 20% Units 2&3 (listening)  
3. 20% Units 4&5 (reading)
4. 20% Units 6&7 (writing)
5. 20% Units 8&9 (speaking)
6. 10% Unit 10 Vocabulary test
*Late submissions of assignments will result in lower scores.

Pass grade as per University of Tsukuba Policy is C or over
Grades: A+ 100 - 90; A 89 - 80; B 79 - 70; C 69 - 60; D 59 - 0

Attending 7 or more lessons is required to earn credit for the course.

その他(受講生にのぞむことや受講上の注意点等)

This course is primarily aimed at M4-M6 students who plan to study or work overseas in healthcare settings.
Students are expected to submit assignments on time, be willing to challenge themselves, and to do their best.
A TOEIC L&R score above 750, or a TOEFL ITP score above 540 (approx. CEFR B2) is recommended as the OET requires a good command of English before one can attempt taking this test.
