授業概要

Medicine and life science have strong connection with social world due to their aspect of practical study. The association between these fields and society have been studied in Science, Technology and Society (STS) study, which is an interdisciplinary field that examines the creation, development, and consequences of science and technology in their historical, cultural, and social contexts. Science communication has been also focused on for facilitating the mutual understanding. In this course, student will study STS & communication in Medicine and life science from its basic principle to practical study. This course is an online joint course between National Cheng Kung University and University of Tsukuba.

授業の到達目標（学修成果）

Medicine and life science have strong connection with society due to their aspect of practical study. The association between these fields and society have been studied in Science, Technology and Society (STS) study, which is an interdisciplinary field that examines the creation, development, and consequences of science and technology in their historical, cultural, and social contexts. Science communication has been also focused on for facilitating the mutual understanding. In this course, student will study STS & communication in Medicine and life science from its basic principle to practical study. This course is an online joint course between National Cheng Kung University and University of Tsukuba.
Through this course, students are expected to achieve the following goals.
The students will be able to explain the principle of STS & communication in medicine and life sciences.
The students will be able to explain interdisciplinary approach to the issues in medicine and life sciences.
The students will be able to explain medicine and life science from the viewpoint of society side.

授業計画

This course is conducted in English as joint online course between National Cheng Kung University and University of Tsukuba. In 2023 academic year, the course will be held in Fall C (The date will be announced latter).

The following is the detail course schedule.
1. Ideas and practice: Introducing Society, Technology and Medicine  (Hsiu-Yun Wang (NCKU), Ryosuke Ohniwa)
2. The emergence of Nobel prize-level research topics in science community (Bei-Chang Yang (NCKU), Ryosuke Ohniwa)
3. Understanding Medicine: Cultural Perspective (Joel Stocker (NCKU), Ryosuke Ohniwa)
4. Understanding research activity in medicine and life science: Scientometrics (Ryosuke Ohniwa)
5. Science Communication 1: Thinking and communication in medicine (Bryan Mathis)
6. Science Communication 2: Science Visualization (Makoto Kobayashi)
7. Science Communication 3: Science Fiction and Manga Creation Thinking (Takahiro Morio)
8. Imagining what a COVID museum will teach us in 50 years (Harry Yi-Jui Wu (NCKU), Ryosuke Ohniwa)
9. A Dialogue Between a Traditional Japanese Mode of Thinking and Scientific Modes of Thinking (Ryosuke Ohniwa)
10. Scientific Visualization: The Philosophical Perspective (Yin Chung Au (NCKU), Ryosuke Ohniwa)
The link to join in the lectures will be shared through MANABA later.
