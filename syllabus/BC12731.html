授業概要

This course is an introduction to development economics, covering both theoretical and empirical research related to development. The course will cover many of the key topics in development: poverty and inequality, industrialization,rural sector development,human capital,governance and institution,among others.

授業の到達目標（学修成果）

This course is a non-technical introduction to development economics. The course will discuss key concepts and issues of economic development.  Students will learn how tools of economics are applied to understanding of the problems of development, and how data and empirical evidence can shed light on conflicting views and questions about the process of development. The class will be conducted in English.

履修者は、このこれらの学習成果により、汎用コンピテンスのうち、汎用コンピテンス：データ・情報リテラシー、広い視野と国際性を、さらに専門コンピテンスのうち、社会科学の素養、社会問題の発見、社会的事象の理解・分析に関する知識や技能を身につけることができる。

授業計画

 Textbook chapters and PPT files will be uploaded in “manaba” course page. You are expected to study the material ahead of each class.
 During each class, I will explain key points from the topic of the week during the 1st half of the class. The assumption is that you have read the material before the class.
 2nd half of the class will be allocated to Q&A and also group work. Students will be divided into groups where you will discuss assigned topics/problems.
 Each week, after the class, students will individually submit a short review paper based on the group work. Feedback to the submitted papers will be given at the beginning of the following class.

学修時間の割り当て及び授業外における学修方法

How to Study
 Read the textbook chapters and the presentation PPT files before the class.
 Make a list of questions you may want to clarify during the class.
 Actively participate in the class. If you have a question, make sure to raise it during the class, or if class time is limited, upload your question on the “manaba” thread.
 During the group work, participate constructively, helping each other in understanding the material.
 After each class, review the class and submit the “review paper” by specified deadlines.  
 Prepare for the final examination by reviewing all the materials discussed in the class.
 If you have a particular interest in some topics, try reading the academic journal articles and other materials referenced in each textbook chapter.

For each class hour, you will need to study at least 1.5 hours for preparation and review.
(各授業1時限分の前後において、それぞれ1.5時間分の自習を必要とする。)
