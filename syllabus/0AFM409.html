授業概要

This course provides a general introduction to the field of International Relations (IR), International Political Economy (IPE) and major themes in world politics. It is designed to introduce students to IR and IPE by encompassing various approaches. The course will cover basic theories as well as empirical coverage of core aspects of current international relations, including sources of conflict and cooperation, the role of non-state actors, and contemporary global issues.

Course Objectives(Learning Outcomes)

The learning goals of this course are:
1. to understand the major theories and approaches in the discipline of IR and IPE
2. to critically evaluate and apply such theories and approaches
3. to obtain knowledge of major themes in IR and IPE
4. to learn about the major international and regional institutions in world politics as well as significant developments in world politics
5. to apply theories to existing case studies as well as critical thinking towards the case studies

Course Keywords

International Relations (IR), International Political Economy (IPE), Global Political Economy (GPE), security, regionalism

Grading Philosophy

1. Active Participation (40% of total grade).
Students are expected to actively attend class. The student will be expected to read the assigned chapters and be prepared for discussions in smaller group activities, which is one of the important factors of class participation.
2. Presentation (30% of total grade)
Students need to make a 15-min presentation. The presentation also needs to be intended to raise questions to further discussion after the presentation
3. Final written assignment (30% of total grade)
Students need to choose one case study and explain using the theoretical frame- work that are introduced in this course (ca. 2500 words)

Course Hours Breakdown and Out-of-Class Learning

This course basically combines lectures (50%) and group discussion (50%). Day 4 class is composed of student presentations (100%) while Day 5 class combines student presentation (50%), lecture (20%) and group discussion (30%)

Regarding out-of-class learning 1) students should read the assigned reading materials beforehand, 2) submit a short paper answering the discussion questions in the next class
