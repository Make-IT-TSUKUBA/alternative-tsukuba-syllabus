BE22471   Globalization and Development

2.0 単位, 2 - 4 年次, 春AB 金5,6
キンポ ネイサン

授業概要

This course examines the politics of development in the era of globalization, exploring major issues and problems being encountered and confronted by the developing countries in the contemporary period.
The aims of the course are: • To broaden students’ knowledge of major development issues and the impact of globalization on developing countries. • To provide students with a good introduction to globalization theory and to the politics of development.
Academic goal:
To help promote the TISS Program’s objective of developing global citizens who can respond to the needs of a global society.

授業計画

There will be lectures, videos, discussions and debates on globalization theory, major development issues, politics in the developing world, and the impact of globalization on developing countries.

1) Introduction; What is Globalization; Globalization in History (Steger, Ch. 1-2)
2) The Economic and Technological Dimensions of Globalization (Steger, Ch. 3); The Economy in a Global Age (Heywood & Whitham, pp. 143-168 only); The Unfolding Sovereign Debt Crisis (Mosley & Rosendorff)
3) The Political Dimension of Globalization (Steger, Chapter 4); Nations and Nationalism in a Global Age (Heywood & Whitham, Ch. 8)
4) The Cultural Dimension of Globalization (Steger, Ch. 5); Identity and Its Politics (Heywood & Whitham, ch. 9)
5) Ideological Confrontations over Globalization (Steger, Ch. 7); The Backlash against Globalization (Walter); The Globalization of Our Discontent (Stiglitz); Globalization Backlash in Developing Countries (Rudra et al., pp. 2416-2425, 2433-34 only)
6) What Is Development (Goldin, ch. 1); Imperialism and the Colonial Experience (Allina)
7) Poverty, Development and Inequality ((Heywood & Whitham, ch. 16); Paradoxes of Migration and Development (De Haas)
8) How Does Development Happen? (Goldin, ch. 2); The Human Development Approach (Stewart)
9) The Ecological Dimension of Globalization (Steger, Ch. 6); Environmental Issues (Vogler in Baylis et al., 2023, ch. 24); Keys to Climate Action (Bhattacharya et al., ch. 1, pp. 1-19 only)
10) Economic Development in an Era of Climate Change (Chandy); Sustainable Development (Goldin, Ch. 6); Globalization and Development (Goldin, ch. 7) The Future of Development (Goldin, Chapter 8); Present and Future Trends (Steger, Ch. 8).
11) Final exam

履修条件

There are no formal pre-requisites for enrollment into the course.
Students are expected to attend all sessions, study the assigned readings before class, and participate actively in class discussions and debates. Students will be required to participate in two team presentations on topics of previous lectures.

成績評価方法

Participation in class discussions and team presentations: 50% (of overall course mark)
Final exam: 50%.
