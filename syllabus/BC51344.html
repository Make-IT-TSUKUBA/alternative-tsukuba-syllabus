BC51344   English Discussion Seminar (D)

2.0 単位, 1・2 年次, 秋AB 水5,6
CHOTANI MAI

授業概要

The broad purpose of this course is to help students practice their English-language communication skills. During this course, students will have opportunities to develop their discussion skills through individual, pairwork, and group activities. Students are encouraged to engage with other students on an equal basis in a free and open discussion forum.
1.The topics, exercises, and assessment activities may differ according to each instructor.
2.Registration priority will be given to non-native English-speaking students.

備考

国際総合学類生のうち、2024年度入学者と総合学域群からの移行者については別途クラス分けを指示する。原則として、国際総合学類の学生のみ履修可。履修上限は30～40名以内で科目担当者が決定。BC51331を履修済みの学生は履修できない。
英語で授業。
対面

授業方法

講義及び演習

学位プログラム・コンピテンスとの関係

1) General-propose competence: communicative ability, critical and creative thinking skills, broad perspective and internationalism, collaborative, independent & autonomous skills.
2) Special competence: ability of logical expression on international relations, ability of logical expression on international development.

授業の到達目標（学修成果）

The course has three objectives: 1) develop an ability to deliver your own ideas effectively; 2) develop listening and comprehension skills to understand different opinions and ideas; and 3) develop discussion skills through an understanding of English discussion conventions.

キーワード

English, communication, presentation

授業計画

第1回 Course introduction, presenting yourself effectively, getting to know each other
第2回 Short presentation practice and discussions (pair-work) [Topic: Peace and conflict]
第3回 Small-group discussions [Topic: International cooperation]
第4回 Panel discussions [Topic: Social movements conducted on social networks]
第5回 Presentations and discussions with visual aids [Topic: Education]
第6回 Mid-term speech contest
第7回 Workshops and discussions [Topic: Media]
第8回 Poster presentation and small discussions [Topic: Population]
第9回 Discussions and debate [Topic: The environment]
第10回 Final presentation (Term paper)

履修条件

1. The topics, exercises, and assessment activities may differ according to each instructor.
2. Registration priority will be given to non-native English-speaking students.
3.　 Students are expected to prepare thoroughly and to participate actively in class.

成績評価方法

Grades will be based on: class participation, 30%; mid-term speech contest, 30%; final presentation (term paper), 40%.
