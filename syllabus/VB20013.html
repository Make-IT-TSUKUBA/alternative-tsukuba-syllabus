3.0 単位, 2 年次, 春ABC 木5,6,応談
森尾 貴広, サンドバール フェリーぺ

授業概要

This practical training enhances skills of problem formation and solution development through PBL (Problem Based Learning) to attack global issues in the field of “Earth Environment” and “Risk and Security”.
Firstly, students will set individual study project related to global issue. Then, students will conduct their research activities including literature review, data analysis, and survey by consulting with their advisor and mentor. Student will present on their study project and have discussion. This course also provide the lectures of methodology for data analysis related to themes of study project.

備考

学位プログラム・コンピテンスとの関係

In the diploma policy of BPGI, students are required to have the following knowledge and abilities:
(1) A broad knowledge of global issues from the perspective of integrating the humanities and sciences.
(2) Acquire a systematic specialized knowledge of global issues and the ability to analyze them comprehensively from multiple perspectives and to work on solving them with creativity and ingenuity.
(3) The ability to communicate logically and persuasively to express one's own views in a global society, while also being flexible enough to listen to the opinions of others.
(4) Demonstrate leadership in a cross-disciplinary and cross-cultural environment and have the ability to contribute to society.
(5) High ethical standards in a global society and the ability to understand diversity.

This course is designed to cultivate the above competencies (1) (2), (3) and (4).

授業の到達目標（学修成果）

The purposes of undertaking a practical training are to give you:
- An opportunity to enhance skills of problem formation and solution development to attack global issues regarding an area in which you have a special interest.
- An opportunity to learn broad knowledge from the presentation of other students and discussions.
- Experience in managing a project.

<Topic presentation>
Students will have the topic presentation to explain their study project and discuss with faculties and students to develop their idea.
- The topic presentation should include:
 Background, motivation, and relationship with SDGs: It should provide sufficient background information for everyone to understand your study project. What issue, problem, controversy, or important matter has led to research on the topic.
 Aim of study project and research questions (for the 3rd year students): The topic presentation should set out the central aims and questions that will guide your research. You should take time to reflect on the key questions that you are seeking to answer. Research questions should be set considering the time schedule.
 ‘Gap’ in the literature (for the 3rd year students): Outlines the field of literature and the ‘gap’, the main findings to date, theories, debates, and remaining questions within the literature, and explains how the research will contribute to the gap.
 Research Methods (for the 3rd year students): The presentation should outline your research methods, explaining how you are going to conduct your research. Students must include their own analysis results using GIS, statistical method, interview, survey, etc.
 Significance of Research: Students should demonstrate the originality of their intended research. You should therefore explain why your research is important (for example, how your research is expected to contribute to the progress of the research topics, why it is the very time to conduct your research, what impact of your research is expected on solving the global issues concerned, etc.).
- 20 minutes per each student / use slides, videos, etc. + 10 minutes Q&A
- Students have to submit a topic report before the presentation, and it should be a maximum of two pages in length, including references according to the designated format.

<Midterm presentation>
The midterm presentation should summarize the literature review and the project’s progress to date.
- The midterm presentation should include:
 Summary of topic presentation: Background, aim, research questions
 Aim of study project and research questions (for the 2nd year students): The topic presentation should set out the central aims and questions that will guide your research. You should take time to reflect on the key questions that you are seeking to answer. Research questions should be set considering the time schedule.
 Literature review (for the 2nd year students): Outlines the trend of literature and, the main findings to date, theories, debates, and remaining questions within the literature.
 Progress and future plan (for the 3rd year students): Students need to show what you have achieved and how you plan to complete the project. Be specific so that the faculties and other students can assess whether your progress and plan are reasonable.
- 20 minutes per each student / use slides, videos, etc. + 10 minutes Q&A
- Students have to submit a midterm report before the presentation, and it should be a maximum of four pages in length, including references according to the designated format.

<Final presentation>
The final report should be in the style of a journal article with section headings.
- The final presentation should include:
-- Introduction, literature review, methodology, results, discussion and conclusion
-- Systematic literature review and ‘Gap’ in the literature (for the 2nd year students): Outlines the field of literature and the ‘gap’, the main findings to date, theories, debates, and remaining questions within the literature, and explains how the research will contribute to the gap.
-- The answers to the research questions (for the 3rd year students): Appropriate interpretation and discussion of results, and limitations.
- Students have to submit a final report before the presentation, and it should be a maximum of eight pages in length, including references according to the designated format.


<Discussion>
Asking questions and joining in discussions are important parts of practical training. Students learn broad knowledge from the presentations of other students and discussions.
- Students have to submit the question sheets for all presenters.
- Students are strongly recommended to give at least three oral questions during the class.
- Questions and answers that could not be done in class time will be threaded on the manaba forum as necessary.

Third-year students are encouraged to provide advice and assistance to second-year students in the preparation and presentation of their Study Projects, both in and out of class time.

成績評価方法

Each presentation is evaluated as follows.
- Topic presentation 20%
 Common criteria
- Clear introduction of research topic 5%
- Logical organization of report and appropriate citation 5%
- Appropriate response to questions and comments: 5%
 Criteria by year
- Specific and clear aim, research question, gap in literature, and methodology 5% (for the 3rd year students)
- Clear relationship between research topic and global issue 5% (for the 2nd year students)

- Midterm presentation 30%
 Common criteria
- Clear introduction of research topic and aim 10%
- Logical organization of report and appropriate citation 5%
- Appropriate response to questions and comments: 5%
 Criteria by year
- Specific results of current progress 10% (for the 3rd year students)
- Clear introduction literature review including trend and major findings 10% (for the 2nd year students)

- Final presentation 30%.
 Common criteria
- Clear introduction of research topic and aim 5%
- Logical organization of report and appropriate citation 5%
- Appropriate response to questions and comments: 5%
 Criteria by year
- Logical organization of the presentation and appropriate methodology 5% (for the 3rd year students)
- Appropriate interpretation and discussion of results 10% (for the 3rd year students)
- Systematic literature review including trend, methodology, and major findings 10% (for the 2nd year students)
- Research gap and specific research plan 5% (for the 2nd year students)

- Discussion and participation 20%.
 The number of questions will be evaluated.

- The evaluation for students who do not give any presentations will be D.
- The evaluation for students who attend lecture less then 70% will be D.
