授業計画

Angry Women: According to the BBC and other media outlets and journalists, an annual poll by Gallup suggests that women, on average worldwide, have been getting angrier over the past 10 years. Starting from these findings, this class will review the situation of women in diverse countries of the world, such as Afghanistan, Iran, and the home countries of class participants. The main focus of the class is on constructions of “woman” in various uncritical and critical discourses and the construction of “anger” and its gendered mapping. The class will look at various literary and artistic examples for more insight.

第1回 Introduction to the class: BBC on the annual global Gallup Poll of 2022
第2回 Examples of female oppression and rebellion: Afghanistan and Iran
第3回 What is a woman? I (medico-biological, theological, philosophical, and feminist perspectives)
第4回 What is a woman? II (philosophical, post-humanist, and feminist perspectives
第5回 What is anger? I (literary example: “The Book of Jonah”
第6回 What is anger? II (discourses: medico-psychological, theological, ethical,  and feminist)
第7回 Examples from Literature I /Film: Olga Tokarczuk (“Potok")
第8回 Examples from Literature II: Euripides: “Iphigenia at Aulis”
第9回 Examples from Literature III: Elfriede Jelinek
第10回 Outlook
