授業概要

本講義ではライフサイエンス分野におけるバイオハイブリッドシステムの利用による革新的な応用およびそのトレンドを概説する。バイオハイブリッドシステムとは生体由来分子(タンパク質や脂質などの生体分子)と非生体由来物質(合成高分子、両新媒性物質、無機粒子など)からなる共役体や分子集合体のことである。バイオハイブリッドシステムの調製法や機能解析を医薬品やバイオテクノロジー分野への現在・未来の応用例の批評と合わせて説明する。ペプチド・タンパク質が有する様々な機能性を幅広く応用するためのペプチド・タンパク質-ポリマー共役体に関して主に焦点を当てる。

授業の到達目標（学修成果）

The students are expected to become capable of providing a critical overview about the different types of biohybrid systems, how they are prepared, characterized and for which type of applications they are (or might be) useful. Not only the key concepts should be understood, but also some of the details about the ways the biomolecules can be linked to (or assembled with) abiogenic molecules and materials (different possible chemical modifications) and how the biohybrid formation may influence the activity (function) of the biomolecules. At the end of the course, the students should be able to understand, critically evaluate and summarize recent research papers published in scientific journals on the preparation and applications of biohybrid systems.

キーワード

Proteins, synthetic polymers, lipids, inorganic nanoparticles and surfaces, micelles, vesicles

授業計画

The course consists of 10 lectures, each 75 min. The topics presented and discussed in the lectures are scheduled as follows.

(1) Introduction
(2) Overview about the field of biomaterials, with particular focus on the motivation of preparing and applying biohybrid systems. Conceptual classification and presentation of a few selected examples of biohybrid systems from recent literature.
(3) Topic 1: Protein-polymer conjugates, with particular focus on proteins which are catalytically active (enzymes). Discussions involving (i) considerations about the selection of the type of polymers depending on the application (biocompatibility, temperature- or pH-responsiveness, controlled degradability), (ii) the methods of preparing the conjugates (chemical linkage between the proteins and the polymer molecules), (iii) the methods of conjugate purification and analysis, and (iv) current medical and biocatalytic applications and challenges for the future.
(4) Topic 2: Nucleic acid-polymer systems for applications in the field of gene delivery. Limitations and current challenges.
(5) Topic 3: Conjugates and molecular assemblies consisting of iron protoporphyrin IX (FePPIX) and synthetic polymers or amphiphiles. FePPIX is an essential biogenic prosthetic group present in a large number of proteins (e.g., hemoglobin or horseradish peroxidase). Discussion about the preparation and applications of heme-polymer conjugates and heme-containing molecular assemblies and current challenges.
(6) Topic 4: Lipid-polymer and protein-polymer systems, with particular focus on their use for the preparation of vesicles towards applications as drug delivery systems and synthetic organelles. Discussion of challenges for the preparation of cell-like vesicular biohybrid systems (also called “synthetic cells”).
(7) Topic 5: Conjugates between biomolecules and inorganic nanoparticles or surfaces, with special focus on enzymes as the biomolecules. Motivation for such enzyme immobilization and recent examples and challenges for preparing flexible biosensors and enzymatic biofuel cells, both often involving cascade reactions. Short summary of the key concepts discussed in the lectures.
(8) Summary
(9) Group discussion 1
(10) Group discussion 2

During or at the end of the course, each student has to choose a recently published scientific paper in which investigations about a biohybrid system are described, read this paper critically and provide a “paper summary report” ‒ containing chemical structures, figures, tables, schemes (if appropriate) ‒ with the following information. (a) The title and authors of the paper summarized and the information about where the paper was published (journal name, volume, page numbers, year); (b) the type of biohybrid system prepared (chemical structure, scheme); (c) why it was prepared and investigated by the authors; (d) how it was prepared (key steps of the chemical synthesis or assembly procedure); (e) how it was analyzed to make sure that the biohybrid prepared is the one described (methods used); and (f) the key results obtained. Finally, the student should (g) critically comment on strong and weak points of the paper and possibly provide suggestions for improvements or a follow-up work.

履修条件

成績評価方法

The “paper summary report” (approximately two or three A4 pages long) has to be sent to the teacher electronically (as doc file and as PDF) until not later than three weeks after the course finished, together with a PDF of the paper summarized (including Supplementary Information, if applicable).

The “paper summary report” will be evaluated (100 %) according to the following grades: A+ (achieved the goal with excellence); A (achieved the goal with good results); B (achieved the goal); C (achieved the minimum goal); D (did not achieve the goal).

学修時間の割り当て及び授業外における学修方法

The course combines lectures (70 %), group discussions in class (10 %) and out-of-class learning (20 %). Concerning “group discussions”: For each of the five topics presented, the actual lecture will be interrupted with specific questions related to the topics. The questions have to be discussed in small groups first, and then in the entire class. Concerning “out-of-class learning”: Each student has to search on the Internet for existing industrial/commercial applications of the five types of biohybrid systems presented in the lectures, and provide a short document (1 A4 page) with information about (i) the type of biohybrid system, (ii) the actual application, and (iii) the source of the information summarized in the document (website with date of access).
