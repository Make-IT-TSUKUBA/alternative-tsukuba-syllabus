0AB0005   Academic Writing and Research Ethics

1.0 単位, 1 年次, 秋A 木5,6
川﨑 レスリー

授業概要

本科目は、大学院生を対象とした学術論文および研究倫理の入門コースであり、特に初年次修士課程の学生を対象とする。講読と議論を通し、受講生は高い倫理意識に基づく研究デザインの基礎を学ぶ。本科目では、研究計画(主に修士論文)の構築方法、実行可能な研究課題の設定、適切な研究手法の選択、および研究結果の客観的評価と発表方法についても学ぶ。

備考

英語で授業。

授業方法

講義

学位プログラム・コンピテンスとの関係

Knowledge utilization, Research skills, Research ethics

授業の到達目標(学修成果)

By the end of this course, students should acquire the following skills:

1. Understand research design, including respecting research ethics, setting research questions, researching relevant theoretical approaches and frameworks, and devising methodological approaches.
2. Understand the basic components of a thesis and how to integrate relevant literature, theory, methods, data collection, and analysis into a finished master-of-arts-level thesis.
3. Acquire practical knowledge in planning their thesis, including design and writing, as well as basic skills for managing their data collection, data analysis, writing, and formatting.
4. Learn how to appropriately and accurately cite other works in their thesis, paying particular attention to research ethics.

キーワード

Research ethics, Academic writing, Methodology, Master’s thesis

授業計画

In principle, the class meets in the 2nd and 3rd periods on Thursdays in the Fall A semester for five weeks (two class periods per session). A detailed schedule for the class will be distributed via either Manaba or Teams at the first class.

第1回 Class 1
Incorporating research ethics when planning your thesis, approaches to writing

In this class, we will discuss how to incorporate research ethics into each facet of the research project. There will be a class discussion on the merits and demerits of different approaches to writing. Small-group discussion (if time).
Homework: Students will create a research diary focusing on their research projects and do assigned readings.
第2回 Class 2
Research questions, theory, and literature review

In this class, we will learn what constitutes a good research question as well as how to apply theory to research projects. In addition, we will discuss strategies for organizing students’ reading for their literature review. Small-group discussion (if time).
Homework: Students will brainstorm research questions for their theses and do assigned readings.
第3回 Class 3
Methodology and data collection

This class will cover strategies for determining the best methodology for your research project and how to go about collecting data. Qualitative, quantitative, and mixed approaches will be discussed, as well as research ethics in collecting different forms of data. Small-group discussion (if time).
Homework: Students will create a “visual literature review” for their thesis topic and do assigned readings.
第4回 Class 4
Data analysis, reporting, and formatting

Data analysis and interpretation are key components of a thesis. How do you structure the data analysis chapter(s) within your thesis? In this class, we will cover different approaches to analyzing and interpreting data, as well as how to structure your results chapter in your thesis. We will also discuss formatting techniques for the thesis. Small-group discussion (if time).
Homework: Students will complete an exercise in using various expressions to describe data and do assigned readings.
第5回 Class 5
References, citations, and summary

The final class will cover a review of how to write references and cite other research. The latter is particularly important to avoid plagiarism and ensure ethical research standards are maintained. There will also be a brief recap of the previous four classes. Students will be encouraged to ask questions to clarify any points throughout the course.

その他(受講生にのぞむことや受講上の注意点等)

Please keep the following points in mind for this class.

1. Plagiarism policy: If the instructor discovers that an assignment, reflection paper, or any other submitted work for the course has been significantly plagiarism, the student will receive a mark of “zero” for that submitted work and may receive a failing grade in the class.
2. Students are expected to attend regularly and participate actively, including small-group discussions with their peers.
