授業計画

Overshadowed by man-made disasters, ecological and tribalist, this class looks at new theoretical ways to reinvent our concepts of our own lives and of the network planet we are living on. How can a new view of our lives and societies, of our material and discursive foundations help us find solutions for the future? This class focuses on two highly influential and critical thinkers: Donna Haraway and Deborah Bird Rose, and their conceptualizations of “kinship”, “cyborgs”, and earthbound philosophy and ethics.
Basic instruction mode is reading passages and interpreting and discussing them. Students are expected to read and prepare texts (vocabulary) before class and to prepare homework when given and to present it in class. Students are expected to perform actively in class discussions.

成績評価方法

Active performance in classes will be graded and is considered most important (60% of overall grade). Final paper (レポート) (length depending on student's proficiency in English) and/or oral presentation (depending on size of class) will be graded (30% of overall grade).
Homework (when given) and class preparations (connecting learning elements) will be graded (10% of overall grade). Absence from class follows general regulations.

学修時間の割り当て及び授業外における学修方法

The course consists of lectures/reading (30%) and discussions/understanding of material (70%). Students are expected to prepare lessons (homework and otherwise) and to revise learning contents on their own.
